Project1 :

  • The title of this project is "Experimental study of Heat Transfer in impingment of single circular jet on a heated plate."
    • Abstract:

  • The heat transfer characteristics of a single circular air jet impinging normally on a flat plate is studied experimentally.
    The effect of normalized nozzle to plate distance (H/D ratio) and inlet velocity is analyzed for the various mass flow rates of air.
    Calibration of thermocouples and uncertainty analysis is performed before starting the experiments.
    Heat Transfer Coefficient is calculated at every location and compared for various H/D ratios and Reynold’s number.
    The graphs are plotted and the conclusions are drawn.

Project2:

                    
    
                        
  • The titlte of this Project is "Parametric Analysis of flow over Airfoil NACA6412." 
    • 
                        
    
                            
    Abstract:
    
                            
    
                                
  • An airfoil or aerofoil is the cross-sectional shape of a wing, blade (of a propeller, rotor, or turbine), or sail (as seen in cross-section). 
    
                                    In this work, the effect of inlet velocity, the angle of attack, and the inlet turbulence intensity on the lift and drag coefficients during
    
                                    airflow over an airfoil NACA 6412 are studied numerically using ANSYS Fluent. 
    
                                    The study concludes that increasing the angle of attack and inlet velocity increases the thrust force and reduces the drag force on the
    
                                    airfoil. Hence for a higher lift coefficient, a large angle of attack is needed, and for minimum drag small angle of attack is needed. 
     
                                    The effect of turbulence intensity on the drag and lift coefficient is negligible.
